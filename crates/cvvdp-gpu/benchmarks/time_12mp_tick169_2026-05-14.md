## What changed

Tick 168 (`e0b6ca62`):
- Added `fill_f32_kernel` (kernels/pool.rs).
- Added `Cvvdp.baseband_log_l_bkg` pre-allocated handle.
- Band-loop baseband branch now launches the fill kernel into
  the pre-allocated buffer instead of `vec![scalar; n_px]` +
  `create_from_slice`.
- The savings at 12 MP look tiny in isolation (baseband is
  ~700 pixels = 2.8 KB), but combined with tick 169 the JOD
  hot path is now entirely GPU-resident for log_l_bkg data.

Tick 169 (this commit):
- Extracted `_dispatch_ref_weber_pyramid_only(ref_srgb) -> f32`
  and `_dispatch_dist_weber_pyramid_only(dist_srgb) -> ()` from
  `_dispatch_d_bands_into_scratch`. Zero behavior change in
  the existing JOD path.
- The split sets up a future `warm_reference` /
  `compute_dkl_jod_with_warm_ref` fast path: batch scoring
  (one REF, many DIST candidates) becomes literal half the
  weber work because REF weber dispatches once instead of
  once per call.

## Open observations

- weber × 2 = 38.6 ns/px ≈ d_bands = 35.5 ns/px. The CSF + masking +
  pool bucket is sub-noise; the band loop is bandwidth-tight against
  the two weber pyramid passes. d_bands − 2×weber is consistently
  negative in noise — the bucket fits inside weber's variance.
- The 2.26× crossover vs fcvvdp 8-thread is the largest milestone
  in this trajectory. Future perf wins likely require a different
  approach (shared-memory tiling, batch-scoring REF reuse, multi-
  level kernel fusion).
- **Next big lever**: ship the warm-ref API. Real-world batch
  scoring workflows (codec quality sweeps, fixture-based testing)
  could halve their cvvdp-gpu compute cost by dispatching REF
  once and reusing the GPU state across many DIST candidates.
