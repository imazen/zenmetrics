# cvvdp-gpu — 12 MP timing snapshot (tick 156, 2026-05-14)

Run after tick 156 routed the REF weber-pyramid call through
`_dispatch_weber_pyramid_gpu` directly, skipping the bands
readback inside `compute_dkl_weber_pyramid`. Combined with tick
154 (bands_dis split) + tick 155 (REF CSF reads bands_ref
handles), the band loop now has zero host-to-GPU weber uploads.

- Build: `cargo run --release --example time_12mp -p cvvdp-gpu --features cuda`

## What changed

Ticks 154-156 form a sequence:

1. **Tick 154** (`a90b30c2`): Added `bands_dis: Vec<Level>`
   field. `_dispatch_weber_pyramid_gpu` takes a `dest_is_dis: bool`
   parameter; DIST call now writes to `bands_dis`, leaving
   `bands_ref` with REF data through the band loop. CSF reads
   DIST from bands_dis (was bands_ref after the dispatch
   overwrite).

2. **Tick 155** (`d7c7322c`): REF CSF reads `self.bands_ref[k]`
   handles directly. Drops 3 host-to-GPU uploads per non-baseband
   level (~50 MB total).

3. **Tick 156** (this commit): REF weber call goes through
   `_dispatch_weber_pyramid_gpu` directly with manual
   `log_l_bkg`-only readback. Skips the bands readback that
   `compute_dkl_weber_pyramid` would do (~190 MB at 12 MP).

The trajectory of ticks 144-156:
- tick 96:  jod 102 ns/px (1.18× slower than fcvvdp 8t)
- tick 145: jod  87 ns/px (1.01× tied)
- tick 150: jod  75 ns/px (1.15× faster)
- tick 155: jod  70 ns/px (1.22× faster)
- tick 156: jod  60 ns/px (1.43× faster)

## Open observations

- 12 MP jod is now 60 ns/px. The weber pyramid (per side) is
  29 ns/px × 2 = 58 ns/px — almost all of d_bands. The CSF +
  masking + pool overhead is sub-noise.
- Next big target: the weber pyramid itself. Possible: shared-
  memory tiled upscale for the L_bkg expand step, or DRAM
  bandwidth optimization at large sizes.
- The medium→large per-pixel regression (tick 97 finding)
  needs re-measurement — at tick 149 large/medium was 2.2×;
  with most host overhead gone, the GPU-side super-linearity
  may now dominate cleanly.
