([`../docs/JXL_LOSSY_KNOBSPACE_ABLATION_PROGRAM.md`](../docs/JXL_LOSSY_KNOBSPACE_ABLATION_PROGRAM.md)).
**Local-first** (per the picker-pipeline pattern — the Hetzner fleet is for *scale*;
this proves the pipeline + gives the first directional signal).

## Status: PRELIMINARY / question-discovery — NOT shippable calibration

- **n = 12 images** (9 photo / 3 screen), **≤1 MP**, **ssim2 only**, **lean max-dev 1** (each
  knob probed at e7; effort ladder e1–e9 at default knobs). This is Q0 *question-discovery* —
  which knobs to focus on vs deprioritize — **not** a calibration-grade verdict.
- Does NOT yet satisfy the house sweep discipline (≥50/content-class, multi-metric
  butteraugli+cvvdp, full tiny→large size range, line-art + mixed content). Those are the

- Lean caveat (the accepted P0 tradeoff): knobs are measured at **e7 only**, so a knob whose
  payoff is e9-only is invisible here. Revisit under effort×knob if the oracle gap points there.

## Findings (directional)

**Front contributors (share of 421 front points):** effort ladder `e9` 15.4%, `e3`–`e6` (photo-skewed);
`gaborish-off` 10%; the **`k_ac_quant` ladder** kaq0.88/1.0/0.65/0.575 ≈ 22% combined; `strategy`
lean 6.7% / libjxl 1.2%; `faster_decoding` `fd4` 4.3%.

**Content-dependence (the picker signal):**
- `strategy` lean/libjxl cost **~−7.5 ssim2 on screen vs ~−0.5 on photo** (matched-q) — strongly
  content-dependent; the shipping `zen` default is much better on screen content.
- effort ladder + `ans0` + the entropy_mul presets contribute to the **photo** front only.
- `fd4` / `exp` (Experimental mode) also photo-favorable, screen-unfavorable.

**Never on any front (dominated everywhere):** `e1`, `e2`, `prog1` (and `prog2` once).

## Oracle headroom — is a picker worth it? (directional: yes, ~10%)

Per-image best config vs the **best single fixed config** (the gap a perfect per-image picker would
capture), at matched ssim2:

| ssim2 target | best fixed cfg | picker headroom | photo | screen |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 55 | `lean` | **9.9%** bytes | 13.1% | 1.5% |
| 65 | `lean` | **10.3%** | 14.2% | 1.4% |

(High-q targets ≥75 weren't reached by enough configs in this small/≤1 MP corpus — a coverage gap to
fix at scale, not a "no headroom" result.) The per-image-optimal config **varies by image** —
photo→{e4, e9, gab0, lean}, screen→{chroma0, e9, kaq0.88} — so the headroom is genuinely
content-dependent, which is the justification for a picker. **~10% RD headroom, photo-concentrated**
(PRELIMINARY). The goal's "picker within ~1% of oracle" means a trained picker captures all but ~1
point of this ~10% — measurable once the scaled data + picker exist.

## P3 pipeline PROVEN end-to-end + first held-out oracle gap (overfit — needs scale)

Ran the full picker pipeline on the 9 features-covered photo images (the screenshots aren't in the
imazen-26 features TSV): `omni_to_pareto` (4329 pareto rows, 37 configs) → `train_hybrid
--codec-config zenjxl_lossy_dense` (decomposes strategy_knob_flag categorical + effort scalar) →
teacher (per-cell HistGB) → student MLP → gates → 1.46 MB ZNPR-able model. **Held-out split** (train
73 / val 18 decision rows):

| | mean bytes-overhead vs per-row oracle | argmin acc |
|---|---|---|
| Teacher (GBDT) | **7.35%** | 5.6% |
| Student (MLP, deployable) — **val** | **39.2%** | 0.0% |
| Student — train | 3.98% | 52.1% |

The student is **badly overfit** (+35pp train→val) and the trainer's gates correctly fire
`OVERFIT` + `LOW_ARGMIN` + `HIGH_OVERHEAD` + `DATA_STARVED_SIZE` (84 (size,zq) cells with <50 train
rows). This is the expected result at **9 images** — and it's the point: the pipeline works, and the
**~1%-oracle endpoint is gated on scaled, dense training data**, exactly as `DATA_STARVED` says. The
teacher's 7.35% (it sees more structure than the tiny-data student) is the encouraging signal that
the GBDT→MLP distillation can approach the oracle once the data is there. Model + log on /home + Tower
(pointer-filed); NOT a shippable picker.
