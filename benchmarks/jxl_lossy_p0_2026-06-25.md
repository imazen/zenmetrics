## P3 pipeline PROVEN end-to-end + first held-out oracle gap (overfit ‚Äî needs scale)

Ran the full picker pipeline on the 9 features-covered photo images (the screenshots aren't in the
imazen-26 features TSV): `omni_to_pareto` (4329 pareto rows, 37 configs) ‚Üí `train_hybrid
--codec-config zenjxl_lossy_dense` (decomposes strategy_knob_flag categorical + effort scalar) ‚Üí
teacher (per-cell HistGB) ‚Üí student MLP ‚Üí gates ‚Üí 1.46 MB ZNPR-able model. **Held-out split** (train
73 / val 18 decision rows):

| | mean bytes-overhead vs per-row oracle | argmin acc |
|---|---|---|
| Teacher (GBDT) | **7.35%** | 5.6% |
| Student (MLP, deployable) ‚Äî **val** | **39.2%** | 0.0% |
| Student ‚Äî train | 3.98% | 52.1% |

The student is **badly overfit** (+35pp train‚Üíval) and the trainer's gates correctly fire
`OVERFIT` + `LOW_ARGMIN` + `HIGH_OVERHEAD` + `DATA_STARVED_SIZE` (84 (size,zq) cells with <50 train
rows). This is the expected result at **9 images** ‚Äî and it's the point: the pipeline works, and the
**~1%-oracle endpoint is gated on scaled, dense training data**, exactly as `DATA_STARVED` says. The
teacher's 7.35% (it sees more structure than the tiny-data student) is the encouraging signal that
the GBDT‚ÜíMLP distillation can approach the oracle once the data is there. Model + log on /home + Tower
(pointer-filed); NOT a shippable picker.

## Oracle gap CONVERGES with data (the loop works) ‚Äî 2026-06-25 retrain on 60 imgs

Retrained the same pipeline on the completed 60-img scaled sweep (28860 pareto rows, all 60
features-covered; val 136 rows vs 18 at 9 imgs ‚Üí a far more honest held-out estimate):

| training data | student **val** overhead | train overhead | overfit gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| 9 imgs  | 39.2% | 3.98% | +35.2pp |
| **60 imgs** | **17.66%** | 7.36% | +10.3pp |

6.6√ó more data nearly **halved** the held-out oracle gap (39.2‚Üí17.66%) and cut the overfit gap
35‚Üí10pp. Still `DATA_STARVED` (84 (size,zq) cells <50 train rows; most knob cells have 1 member
config) ‚Äî the gate quantifies the remaining data need. This is the loop's core mechanism, measured:
**oracle gap converges toward the target along the data axis.** Reaching ~1% is a data-scale problem
(fleet-scale dense sampling), not a pipeline problem ‚Äî every mechanism is proven. (The per-cell HistGB
teacher degrades to 31.9% on the larger/harder val because each knob cell has ~1 member config; the
pooled MLP student generalizes better here at 17.66% ‚Äî the deployable number.)

## CORRECTION: it's noise + DATA_STARVED, not clean convergence (90-img retrain)

Combined the 60-img local + 30-img fleet omnis (90 unique imgs, all imazen-26) and retrained:

| training data | student **val** overhead |
|---|---|
| 9 imgs (val 18 rows) | 39.2% |
| 60 imgs (val 136) | 17.66% |
| **90 imgs (val 185)** | **36.88%** |

**Non-monotonic** (39‚Üí17.66‚Üí**36.88**) ‚Äî so the earlier "oracle gap converges with data" framing was
**over-reading two noisy points**. The honest reading: at 9‚Äì90 imgs the picker is `DATA_STARVED`
(84 (size,zq) cells <50 train rows in every run) and overfit (+31pp train‚Üíval here), so held-out
overhead is **high-variance (17‚Äì37%)**, not a clean downward trend. Adding the fleet's more-varied
renditions made the small-data fit *harder*, not easier.

**Implication for ‚â§1%:** the endpoint needs the **dense ML-discipline sampling** ‚Äî enough rows *per
(size, zq, content) cell* (the gate's ‚â•50 threshold), via 20 log-spaced sizes √ó dense-q √ó k-means
representative images ‚Äî not merely "more images." That is genuinely a fleet-scale dense-data
generation + multi-round iteration job. The pipeline + fleet are proven; the data protocol is the work.

total image count: ‚â§1% needs the size-stratified dense corpus (‚â•50 rows per size√ózq√ócontent), i.e. a
